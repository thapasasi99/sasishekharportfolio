Seismic Resistance in High-Rise Structures

This comprehensive study examined the behavior of high-rise structures under seismic loads and proposed innovative reinforcement techniques using shape memory alloys and base isolation systems. The research included both computational modeling and physical testing of scaled models.

Key findings demonstrated a 40% improvement in energy dissipation and a significant reduction in structural damage during simulated earthquake conditions. The research was published in the International Journal of Structural Engineering and has been cited in several subsequent studies.

Sustainable Concrete Alternatives

This research focused on developing sustainable concrete mixtures using industrial by-products and locally available materials. We investigated partial replacements of cement with fly ash, slag, and rice husk ash, along with alternative aggregate sources.

Our findings showed that optimized mixtures could reduce the carbon footprint of concrete by up to 35% while maintaining or even improving compressive strength and durability characteristics. The research has practical applications in the Nepalese construction industry, particularly in reducing dependency on imported materials.

Bridge Health Monitoring Systems

This project developed a low-cost wireless sensor network system for continuous monitoring of bridge health parameters. The system utilizes accelerometers, strain gauges, and environmental sensors to detect changes in structural behavior that may indicate damage or deterioration.

Field testing on three bridges in Nepal demonstrated the system's effectiveness in detecting early signs of structural issues. The technology is particularly valuable for developing countries where resources for infrastructure maintenance are limited but safety is paramount.
