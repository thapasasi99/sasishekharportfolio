About Me

Building Excellence in Civil Engineering

I am an undergraduate civil engineer with a strong foundation in structural analysis, pavement engineering, and traffic management. My work combines theoretical knowledge with practical field experience.

Currently, I focus on numerical modeling, finite element analysis, and sustainable pavement design. I have led research teams in conducting complex structural analyses and contributed to real-world engineering projects across Nepal.

My expertise spans ETABS analysis, finite element modeling, laboratory testing, and site investigation. I am passionate about advancing engineering practices through research and innovation.

Engineering & field work

Projects

01

Mechanistic Modeling of Rigid Pavement Subject to Traffic and Environmental Loads

Built a FEM simulation of a 29 ft × 12 ft concrete slab with transverse cracks, validated under wheel loading, temperature effects, and shrinkage. Stress results examined at critical crack faces.

02

Asphalt Mix Performance Evaluation for Sustainable Pavement Design

Laboratory evaluation of aggregate and bitumen properties; Marshall stability and flow tests to assess pavement strength. Supervised by Dr. Pradeep Kumar Shrestha.

03

Traffic Volume Survey and Analysis at Prithivi Highway Intersection

Conducted 24-hour manual traffic counts at CERC Pvt. Ltd., classifying vehicles and identifying peak hours, vehicle composition, and modal share to support transport planning.

04

Road Safety Audit and Pavement Condition Assessment

Investigated factors influencing road accidents by inspecting road surfaces and roadside issues. Analyzed correlations between pavement condition and accident data to identify safety-critical areas.
