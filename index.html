Numerical Modeling, Analysis and Structural Design of G+4 RCBuilding with Re-entrant Corners.

The architectural design of structures like schools, offices, and hotels often features asymmetry and plan irregularities, demanding careful consideration during seismic analysis and structural design. Re-entrant corners with certain conditions in a building are considered a horizontal irregularity by seismic codes such as IS 1893 (Part I):2002. This study focuses on the analysis and design of a multi-storey office building situated in Zone V with a re-entrant corner horizontal irregularity. It encompasses the preliminary design of structural components and seismic analysis using a numerical model based on IS 1893 (Part I):2002, implemented in ETABS v20.0.0. Key responses, including time period, modal mass participation ratios, and storey drift ratio, are assessed to ensure compliance with codal requirements. Following this, structural design for beams, columns, and slabs is executed, and the required longitudinal and transverse reinforcement in different locations of these components is compared across various floors.

Mechanistic Modeling of Rigid Pavement Subject to Traffic and Environmental Loading

This was a finite element modeling (FEM) project that involved simulating a 29 ft × 12 ft concrete slab with 10 in. thickness, incorporating transverse cracks spaced at 4.5 ft, and a 2 ft segment at the center. Leveraging symmetry, only half the slab (29 ft × 6 ft) was modeled. A 9000-pound wheel load was applied at a critical crack location to evaluate structural response. Concrete and rebar were modeled with solid and wedge elements, respectively, and interface elements captured rebar-concrete bonding, crack interactions, and subgrade support with realistic stiffness and slip conditions. The model was validated under wheel loading, temperature effects, and shrinkage, with stress results closely examined at critical crack faces....

Asphalt Mix Performance Evaluation for Sustainable Pavement Design

Laboratory tests were conducted on aggregates and bitumen to evaluate their suitability for pavement construction. The abrasion and crushing value tests on aggregates measured resistance to wear and compressive strength, ensuring durability under traffic loading. For bitumen, penetration, viscosity, ductility, and softening point tests were performed to assess consistency, flow behavior, elasticity, and temperature susceptibility of the binder. In addition, Marshall stability and flow tests were carried out on bituminous mixes to determine their load-bearing capacity and deformation characteristics, providing a clear understanding of pavement strength and overall performance.
